Usage

Searching for a car is as easy as: !autotrader search cars You can also search for bikes and vans.
Finding a vehicle that meets your specific tastes is a little more involved but is still very straightforward!
If you're looking to find a new Tesla, you'd type the following: !autotrader search cars --make Tesla --condition New The words prefixed with double hyphens (--) are called flags and there are a bunch of them that you can use to narrow down your search.
The following is a complete list of all the different options supported by AutoTrader Bot and their meaning:

Flag Definition
--make The make of the vehicle (e.g. Tesla, Ford, Nissan)
--model The model of the vehicle (e.g. Model S, Focus, Skyline)
--variant The variant of the vehicle (e.g. ST, EcoBoost)
--min-year --max-year The minimum or maximum year of manufacture
--min-price --max-price The minimum or maximum price (in Â£) to search for
--results The amount of results to get from the search
--postcode The postcode to center the search around
--radius The distance in which to search from the given postcode
--condition The condition of the vehicle; either new, used or nearly new
--min-mileage --max-mileage The minimum or maximum mileage done by the vehicle
--min-cc --max-cc The minimum or maximum cc of the vehicle (only applies to bikes)
--body The body type of the vehicle (i.e. SUV or Coupe)
--fuel-type The type of fuel used by the vehicle (petrol or diesel)
--gearbox The type of gearbox that vehicle uses (either manual or automatic)
--colour The colour of the vehicle
--cab The cab type of the vehicle (only applies to vans)
--wheelbase The wheelbase of the vehicle (only applies to vans)
--min-engine-size --max-engine-size The minimum or maximum size of the vehicle's engine (i.e. 1.2)
--drivetrain The drivetrain of the vehicle (i.e. Four Wheel Drive)
--doors The number of doors that the vehicle has
--min-seats --max-seats The minimum or maximum number of seats that the vehicle has

NOTE: If your input contains a space (e.g. Range Rover), put some quotes around it so it's understood as a single value (e.g. "Ranger Rover")
